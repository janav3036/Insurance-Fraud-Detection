Feature Engineering

To improve fraud detection performance, several derived variables were created from the raw dataset. These engineered features capture financial relationships, behavioral patterns, and claim characteristics that may indicate suspicious insurance activity.

1. Claim to Vehicle Ratio

Formula: total_claim / vehicle_price

This feature measures how large a claim is relative to the value of the vehicle. Fraudulent claims often involve inflated damage amounts compared to the vehicle's actual value.

2. Injury to Claim Ratio

Formula: injury_claim / total_claim

This captures the proportion of the claim attributed to injury-related costs. Exaggerated injury claims can sometimes indicate fraudulent activity.

3. Income to Claim Ratio

Formula: total_claim / annual_income

A claim amount that is extremely large relative to a policyholder’s income may indicate financial motivation for fraudulent behavior.

4. Deductible to Claim Ratio

Formula: policy_deductible / total_claim

This feature measures how significant the deductible is compared to the total claim amount. Fraudulent claims sometimes attempt to maximize payouts above deductible thresholds.

5. Repeat Claim Flag

A binary feature that identifies claimants with more than two previous claims. Frequent claims may increase the likelihood of suspicious activity.

6. Days Open Flag

This flag identifies claims that remain open longer than the dataset median. Extended claim durations may indicate investigations, disputes, or complex claim behavior.

7. Claim Severity Index

Formula: total_claim / (policy_deductible + 1)

This feature combines claim size and deductible value to measure the relative severity of a claim compared to the insured policy structure.

8. High Claim Flag

A binary indicator for unusually large claims based on the upper percentile of claim values in the dataset. Large claims tend to have a higher probability of containing fraudulent activity.
