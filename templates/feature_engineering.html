Feature Engineering

To improve fraud detection, several derived features were created from the raw dataset. These engineered variables capture behavioral and financial patterns that indicate any suspiscious claims.

Claim to Vehicle Ratio

Formula: total_claim/vehicle_price

This feature measures how large a claim is relative to the value of the vehicle. Fraudulent claims often involve inflated damage amounts compared to the vehicle'a actual value.

Injury to Vehicle Ratio

Formula: injury_claim/total_claim

This captures the proportion of the claim attributed to injury. Exaggerated injury costs can be a common indicator of insurance fraud.

Income to Claim Ratio

Formula: total_claim/annual_income

A very large claim compared to a policyholder's annual income may indicate financial motivation for fraudulent activity.

4. Deductible to Claim Ratio

Formula: policy_deductible / total_claim

This shows how significant the deductible is relative to the claim amount. Fraudulent claims often try to maximize payouts above the deductible threshold.

5. Repeat Claim Flag

This binary feature identifies claimants with more than two past claims. Frequent claim history can increase fraud risk.

6. Days Open Flag

Claims that stay open for longer than the dataset median may indicate complications, investigations, or suspicious activity.

7. Claim Severity Index

Formula: total_claim / (policy_deductible + 1)

This feature combines claim size with deductible value to capture the relative severity of the claim.

8. High Claim Flag

A binary indicator for unusually large claims based on the upper distribution of claim amounts. Large claims have a higher probability of containing fraudulent behavior.
